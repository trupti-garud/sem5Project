Kolhapuri Chappals Gallery

Kolhapuri chappals are Indian decorative hand-crafted and braided leather slippers that are locally tanned using vegetable dyes. Kolhapuris are a style of open-toed, T-strap sandal, but also braided leather Mules or shoe-type designs are common.

History

The origin of Kolhapuri Chappals dates back to the 12th century when King Bijjala and his prime minister Basavanna encouraged production to support local cordwainers. Previously known as Kapashi, Paytaan, Kachkadi, Bakkalnali, and Pukri, the name indicated the village where they were made. Shahu I of Kolhapur encouraged the Kolhapuri Chappal industry, and 29 tanning centers were opened during his rule.

Production and Market

Kolhapuris were originally made from buffalo-hide and thread. They were durable despite the extreme heat and mountainous terrain in Maharashtra. Artisans perform several operations to make them, such as pattern making, cutting, stitching, and polishing. In 2020, the market was estimated at ₹9 crore, with over 10,000 artisans working in Kolhapur. About 30% of the six lakh pairs produced annually are exported.

Design & Market Trends

In the 1970s, with the Hippie movement, Kolhapuri chappals became popular in the U.S. A French brand, CHAPPAL, exports these sandals to France in various colors and designs.
